Gourad vs Phong shading
Gourad shades each vertex and then calculates each pixel color by interpolating across vertices. While this significantly reduces the amount of calculations, it can cause some unnatural lighting effect, which is especially seen in highlights. Highlights are "localized" effect, meaning they effect a rather small portion of the mesh at time. With few vertices, these highligts can spread unnaturally to nearby vertices due to interpolation (as seen in previous part).
In Phong shading the highlights is calculated for each pixel giving a much smoother and realistic look. Phong is significantly more processor heavy, and as such can be slower. However, if you want to achieve the same effect using Gourad, you'd have to increase the vertex numbers by a lot (as seen in previous part). If you want realistic highlights, and your object relies on interpolation and not vertices to look good (as this sphere does), Phong is the best option.
